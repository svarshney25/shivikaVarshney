For my final project in AP Computer Science A, I worked with a few
classmates to code a game called Island Adventures! There are 4 maps: jungle,
beach, tundra, and lava. The goal of the game is to accumulate upgrades, boosts,
and currency in order to increase player stats and defeat the bosses of each new island.
This is achieved when the player adequately interacts with its surroundings
and wins mini-games, passes obstacles, collects currency, opens chests, and more.
With each successful action, the player receives either a boost or some amount
of coins, which are then used in the shop catalog to upgrade weapons and amenities.
At the end of the last island, a final boss (my teacher) is presented and
the game is only completed after its defeat. Java GUI was used to build the game.

I co-founded a nonprofit, Code Her World with my friend! As an organization,
we provide FREE virtual programming classes to girls and nonbinaries from the
age of 11-18. We've hosted various camps and workshops for all sorts
of computer science skills like Web Development and app design.
We've also launched a 1:1 mentorship program where students have the
opportunity to receive personalized instruction in computer science. Whether it's
homework help or learning the basics of a new language, we've got them covered!
We have taught over 50 students and have reached over 100 students across
7 countries! With 15 people on our team, we have been widening our impact and
hosting more programs. Our website was programmed by the other co-founder and
I using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Through the Inspirit AI program, I worked with a group of
other students to develop an artificial intelligence model that detects objects for
self-driving cars. Images were first trained to be classified as either a truck, car, or
person. We used 3 different techniques to train the images: perceptrons and sliding
windows, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and transfer learning, and YOLO v3.
Yolo generated the highest accuracy. The program was 10 weeks long
and each week, we explored various aspects of AI and the math
behind it (linear regression and k-nearest neighbors).

Impactive was an app created for the Congressional App Challenge.
The purpose of the app is to help small businesses and nonprofits design
apps for their organizations without having to spend thousands of dollars on
hiring a professional. They would be able to design as well as learn
how to develop their own app through free tutorials to help them get started.
We used the Swift programming language and the XCode IDE to build it.
With this app, my partner and I placed 3rd in our congressional district!

At the annual TecHacks Hackathon, my group and I won the Activism Track
for developing a website called Menstruation Station. The website is a
"mock" nonprofit organization that provides underserved women and girls
with free menstrual products. As a way to obtain more products than the usual,
they can complete community service hours. Additionally, through
the website other people can educate themselves on the issue, donate
a certain amount towards the cause, or initiate their own chapter locally. As
a challenge, we added a login/signup screen. That portion took a
while to complete because it was a brand new topic to us. We used HTML, CSS,
and JavaScript to develop the website.
